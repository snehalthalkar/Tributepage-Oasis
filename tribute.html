Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India , People's President"

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, (born October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India—died July 27, 2015, Shillong), was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

He spent four decades of his life as a researcher and science head, primarily at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and was personally required in India's civilian space program and military rocket advancement efforts.

Kalam had a humble beginning and came from a financially challenged family. He was a bright student and excelled in academics. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and went on to become one of the most respected scientists in India. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and missile development efforts, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Abdul Kalam passed away in 2015 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His death was mourned by people from all over the world, and he is remembered as a true visionary, a great scientist, and a people's President. His life and legacy continue to inspire millions of people, especially young students, to pursue their dreams and make a positive impact on society.
